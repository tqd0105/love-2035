# Project 2035 Specification

## 1. Purpose

Project 2035 is a long-term digital memory platform designed to preserve the story of a relationship.

The system acts as a structured archive for:

- shared memories
- personal letters
- wedding events
- guest interactions
- private emotional content

The platform must remain stable and accessible for many years, with the goal of preserving memories until at least 2035.

The system prioritizes emotional continuity and data preservation over rapid feature growth.

---

## 2. Core Vision

Project 2035 is not a social network.

It is a curated digital memory space designed for:

- the couple
- their family
- their wedding guests
- future reflection

The platform should function as both:

1. A living timeline during the relationship.
2. A wedding experience portal during the event.
3. A long-term archive afterward.

---

## 3. Primary Capabilities

### 3.1 Timeline

A chronological timeline documenting relationship milestones.

Examples:

- first meeting
- anniversaries
- important life events
- wedding milestones

Timeline entries may include:

- text
- photos
- media
- tags
- visibility rules

---

### 3.2 Letter System

A private or semi-private letter system allowing emotional messages to be preserved.

Letters may support:

- time-lock (open in future)
- password lock
- read tracking
- mood tags

Letters serve as a personal emotional archive.

---

### 3.3 Media System

Media content associated with memories.

Examples:

- photos
- short videos
- audio messages

Media may appear in:

- timeline
- letters
- profiles
- wedding albums

Media storage must be scalable and easily exportable.

---

### 3.4 Wedding Experience

During wedding events the platform becomes an interactive guest portal.

Guests may:

- view the story timeline
- access wedding information
- upload photos
- send wishes
- view curated albums

The platform should support QR code access during the event.

---

### 3.5 Guest Interaction

Guests can interact with the system in controlled ways.

Possible interactions:

- leaving wishes
- uploading photos
- viewing selected memories
- RSVP participation

Guest actions must respect privacy boundaries.

---

### 3.6 Private Vault

A highly private storage area for content visible only to the couple.

Examples:

- time capsule letters
- deeply personal messages
- sensitive memories

Vault content must support stronger protection mechanisms.

---

## 4. System Modes

The system operates under three primary modes.

### RELATIONSHIP

Default mode during the relationship.

Features:

- timeline growth
- letters
- media uploads
- private vault

---

### WEDDING

Activated during the wedding event.

Features:

- public timeline access
- guest interactions
- QR code access
- wedding albums
- guest wishes

Editing of core memories may be restricted during this period.

---

### ARCHIVE

Long-term preservation mode.

Features:

- read-only timeline
- preserved letters
- wedding memories locked
- no new content creation

The system becomes a stable memory archive.

---

## 5. User Roles

The platform recognizes four primary user roles.

### ADMIN

System owner responsible for configuration and moderation.

Capabilities include:

- system configuration
- guest approval
- mode switching
- moderation actions

---

### COUPLE

Primary users of the platform.

Capabilities include:

- creating memories
- writing letters
- managing vault content
- uploading media
- editing timeline

---

### APPROVED_GUEST

Guests with extended access permissions.

Capabilities may include:

- viewing private timeline sections
- leaving wishes
- participating in certain interactions

---

### PUBLIC_VISITOR

General visitors.

Capabilities limited to:

- viewing public content
- accessing wedding information

---

## 6. Visibility Model

Content visibility is controlled by layered access levels.

Possible visibility levels include:

- PUBLIC
- APPROVED_GUEST
- COUPLE
- PASSWORD_LOCKED

This system ensures emotional privacy while allowing selective sharing.

---

## 7. Data Longevity Principles

The system must prioritize long-term durability of memories.

Key principles:

- avoid proprietary formats
- allow periodic export
- support static archival snapshots
- maintain backward compatibility

Data must remain recoverable even if hosting infrastructure changes.

---

## 8. Archive Philosophy

Memories should not be aggressively edited after they become part of the historical record.

The system should preserve:

- context
- chronology
- authenticity

Archive mode represents stabilization rather than shutdown.

---

## 9. Success Criteria

The system is considered successful if:

- the couple can revisit their timeline years later
- wedding memories remain accessible
- guest contributions are preserved
- the platform remains stable and maintainable

Longevity is more important than feature complexity.

---

## 10. Guiding Principle

Project 2035 is designed to protect meaningful moments.

Technology is only a tool.

Memory continuity is the real goal.
